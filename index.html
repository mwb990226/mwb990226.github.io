Why it matters

Genome editing is rapidly advancing across ex vivo and in vivo therapeutic programs, but safety assessment is now a dominant bottleneck. Existing approaches often provide an incomplete view of genome-wide outcomes, especially for rare, complex, and delayed events that can shape clonal behavior and long-term stability.

Problem

Snapshot safety is incomplete

Many standard assays emphasize predicted or partial off-target signals and can miss low-frequency, high-impact outcomes.

Result: uncertainty in IND-enabling studies and early clinical translation.
Solution

Rare outcome visibility + interpretation

We focus on detecting rare and complex editing outcomes and connecting them to biologically meaningful, decision-ready signals.

Result: clearer risk attribution and mechanistic context.
Impact

Regulatory-aligned decision support

Our infrastructure is designed to help teams answer practical questions: what happened, how confident are we, and what does it mean for development?

Result: stronger, more interpretable safety packages.
