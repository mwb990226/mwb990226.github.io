Genome editing safety measurement infrastructure

From detection to risk interpretation

EditVerity builds regulatory-grade measurement systems to detect, quantify, and interpret rare and complex genome editing outcomes—and to distinguish neutral events from development-relevant risk through multi-layer context.

We integrate outcome measurement across DNA, chromatin, RNA, and cellular layers to support program decisions from discovery optimization through IND-enabling studies.

What we do

EditVerity builds measurement infrastructure to improve visibility into genome editing outcomes. Our platform is designed to operate at the limits of detection, where rare and unintended events can have disproportionate biological and translational impact. We support both ex vivo and in vivo programs across research and translational stages.

Platform

A modular safety measurement platform spanning genome editing outcomes, genome stability, functional consequences, clonal behavior, and immunogenicity—paired with contextual interpretation so results are decision-relevant, not just variant lists.

Platform module

Off-target profiling

Multiple orthogonal methods for mapping genome-wide off-target edits with high sensitivity and artifact control. (This module focuses on off-target mapping and does not include structural variant analysis.)

Typical outputs: off-target edit sites, edit spectra, VAF estimates, reproducibility controls.
Platform module

Chromatin integrity

Structural variant (SV) and genome stability assessment—including chromosomal abnormalities, insertions/deletions, and risks of oncogenicity or insertional mutagenesis.

Typical outputs: SV calls, large-scale rearrangements, genome instability signatures, insertional risk signals.
Platform module

Functional consequence analysis

On- and off-target consequence profiling across transcriptomic and epigenetic layers to connect edits to functional impact.

Typical outputs: pathway-level hazard signals, dysregulation signatures, regulatory impact summaries.
Platform module

Clonal fitness & expansion risk

Assessment for clonal expansion and/or unregulated proliferation of edited cells, including longitudinal selection behavior.

Typical outputs: clonal dynamics, selection trends, proliferation advantage signals, stability over time.
Platform module

Genomic variant interpretation

Contextual classification of detected variants—recognizing that not all off-targets or variants are harmful. We integrate multi-layer evidence to distinguish neutral/benign outcomes from development-relevant risk.

Typical outputs: risk tiering, evidence summaries, context-aware interpretation reports.
Platform module

Immunogenicity assessment

Assessment of immunogenicity of genome-editing components and expressed transgene(s) using standard analytical approaches.

Typical outputs: immunogenicity risk flags, component/transgene assessment summaries, supporting evidence.

Applications

From discovery through translational stages, we clarify outcome risk, mechanism, and decision relevance.

IND / pre-IND safety packages

  • Outcome profiling with high sensitivity and artifact control
  • Genome stability assessment (SVs and chromosomal abnormalities)
  • Contextual interpretation aligned to development and safety questions

Ex vivo & in vivo programs

  • Ex vivo edited cell products: clonal fitness, stability, and expansion risk
  • In vivo editing: sparse and heterogeneous outcome profiling
  • Comparative profiling across CRISPR, base editing, and prime editing

Modality benchmarking

  • Controlled comparisons across modalities and delivery strategies
  • Consistent sensitivity, controls, and interpretation framework
  • Decision support for modality selection and optimization

Longitudinal monitoring

  • Track stability, selection, and delayed consequences over time
  • Evaluate clonal behavior and proliferation advantage signals
  • Support comparability and durability assessment strategies

Technology

Our platform combines rare-event visibility, artifact control, and biologically grounded interpretation to produce outcome profiles that are decision-relevant—not just variant lists.

Technology

Rare-event visibility

Experimental strategies designed to detect ultra-low frequency genome editing outcomes, including complex repair products.

Technology

Artifact control

Methods to distinguish true biological variants from PCR and sequencing artifacts, enabling confident interpretation at low variant allele frequencies.

Technology

Multi-layer integration

Coordinate genomic outcomes with transcript-level and cellular readouts when needed to connect events to functional consequences.

Regulatory

We design measurement outputs to support development decisions and regulatory documentation—focused on clarity, traceability, and interpretability.

Alignment & reporting

  • Decision-relevant reporting structure (not raw variant dumps)
  • Context-aware interpretation and risk tiering
  • Transparent controls and documentation of assay boundaries

Validation approach

  • Sensitivity, specificity, and reproducibility characterization
  • Benchmarking and comparability frameworks
  • Clear assumptions, limitations, and data provenance
