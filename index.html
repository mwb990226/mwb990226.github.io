Genome editing outcome measurement

Safety infrastructure for genome editing outcomes

EditVerity develops experimental and analytical systems to detect, quantify, and interpret rare and complex genome editing outcomes that are not captured by standard assays.

We focus on low-frequency and structurally complex events—including rare indels, large deletions, inversions, translocations, and chromothripsis-like rearrangements—and provide interpretation across DNA, RNA, and cellular layers to support development decisions.

What we do

EditVerity builds measurement infrastructure to improve visibility into genome editing outcomes. Our systems are designed to operate at the limits of detection, where rare and unintended events can have disproportionate biological and translational impact. We support both ex vivo and in vivo programs across research and translational stages.

Core capabilities

We combine rare-event visibility, artifact control, and biologically grounded interpretation to produce outcome profiles that are decision-relevant—not just variant lists.

Capability

Rare outcome detection

Experimental strategies designed to enrich and detect ultra-low frequency genome editing outcomes, including complex repair products and structural variants.

Typical applications: rare indels, large deletions, inversions, translocations, complex rearrangements.
Capability

Error-suppressed sequencing

Methods to distinguish true biological variants from PCR and sequencing artifacts, enabling confident interpretation at very low variant allele frequencies.

Typical applications: sensitivity benchmarking, mosaic editing, rare-event confirmation.
Capability

Outcome interpretation

Analytical frameworks that translate outcome profiles into biologically and development-relevant insights, rather than isolated off-target site lists.

Typical applications: safety assessment, modality comparison, program decision support.
Extension

Longitudinal assessment

Track outcome stability and selection over time to evaluate durability, clonal behavior, and delayed consequences.

Typical applications: stability, selection, clonal dynamics, time-course studies.
Extension

Multi-layer context

Coordinate genomic outcomes with transcript-level or cellular readouts when needed to link rare events to functional consequences.

Typical applications: mechanistic interpretation, phenotype linkage, program risk mapping.
Extension

Benchmarking & comparability

Controlled comparisons across modalities, delivery strategies, and conditions with consistent sensitivity and interpretation.

Typical applications: modality selection, optimization, comparability assessments.

How we work

What we provide

An integrated safety measurement platform for genome and cell therapies.

We provide a comprehensive infrastructure to detect, quantify, and interpret genome editing–associated risks across molecular and cellular layers. Our platform integrates:

  • Off-target and unintended editing outcome scanning
  • Genome structural variant and large-scale rearrangement detection
  • Epigenetic and chromatin state profiling
  • Transcriptional and regulatory impact assessment
  • 3D genome architecture and long-range interaction analysis
  • Cell state and functional behavior assays
Together, these measurements generate an integrated safety profile that supports program-level decision-making—from discovery optimization to IND-enabling studies.

What we do not emphasize

  • Not a menu of disconnected assays
  • Not limited to predicted off-target sites
  • Not a one-off report without interpretive context
Our emphasis is on reusable measurement systems rather than isolated, single-use deliverables.

About

EditVerity Sciences, Inc. was founded to address a growing gap in genome editing: the lack of reliable, system-level methods to detect and interpret rare and unintended outcomes that can shape safety and translational decisions.
